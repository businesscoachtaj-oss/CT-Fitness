Section 01 — Foundation

Your Brand & Story

You were overweight. You fixed it. That's not a backstory — that's your entire marketing strategy. No one can compete with lived experience.

Your unfair advantage

Hormozi calls this "pain to profit." You lived the problem, solved it, and now you sell the solution. Every young guy aged 18–30 who's struggling with his body sees himself in you. That emotional connection is worth more than any marketing budget.

"I was overweight, had no idea what I was doing in the gym, and felt embarrassed every time I walked in. Now I coach young guys to do what I did — but faster, and without the years of wasted effort I went through."

Your niche in one sentence

"I help guys aged 18–30 who are overweight or skinny-fat build their first real physique — through strength training, smart nutrition, and a coach who's been exactly where they are."

Specific enough to attract the right person and repel everyone else. "I help people get fit" gets ignored. This sentence stops a 24-year-old dead mid-scroll.

Know your avatar better than he knows himself

Hormozi: if you can describe someone's problem better than they can, they automatically assume you have the solution. Your guy:

  • 18–30, working or at uni, limited time and energy
  • Has tried the gym before, quit within 8 weeks
  • Watches YouTube fitness but feels more confused, not less
  • Ashamed to take his shirt off, avoids photos, cancels beach trips
  • Wants to look good but doesn't know where to actually start
  • Has told himself "I'll start Monday" more times than he can count
  • Deep down wants someone to just tell him exactly what to do

Brand name direction — keep it personal right now

You're not a faceless app. You're a real person who transformed. That's the product. Pick a name and move — it becomes real through the content, not the name.

Section 06 — Growth

Getting Clients

Hormozi's first dollar came from a personal conversation — not an ad, not a post. Your first 3 clients are already in your phone right now.

Phase 1 — warm outreach (weeks 1–4, zero cost)

People who've commented on your fitness posts. Mates who've asked how you lost the weight. Anyone who's ever said "you should train me." Goal: message 30 people, convert 3. That's 10% — very achievable with a personal message.

Hey [Name], launching my online coaching and taking on a small group of first clients for my 12-week program. Thought of you specifically because [personal reason — e.g. "you mentioned wanting to sort your training", "I know you've been trying to lose weight"]. I coach young guys on strength training and nutrition — I went from being overweight myself to where I am now, and that's exactly what I build programs around. Taking on only 5 people for this first round to keep it personal. Want me to send through the details?

Why this message works

  • You're not asking for money — just "do you want details?" Low friction yes
  • The specific personal reason makes it feel genuine, not a mass pitch
  • "Only 5 people" creates urgency without being salesy
  • Your story is the credibility — no need to list qualifications

Phase 2 — referral system (month 2 onward)

When a client hits a milestone — a PR, visible progress, real confidence — that's the moment to ask:

"Mate, I'm really stoked with how you're going. If you know anyone who'd benefit from this, I'd love an intro — I'll give you a free month for any referral who signs up."

One happy client who refers two people has covered your marketing cost indefinitely.

Phase 3 — inbound via content (month 3+)

Consistent content + a clear offer = inbound DMs. The goal is that within 90 days people are coming to you instead of you chasing them. See the Content Strategy section for the exact system.

Section 07 — Growth

Sales & Closing

The CLOSER framework from Hormozi. Not manipulation — a structure that helps people make a decision that's actually good for them.

When someone DMs — get them on a call first

Don't send a PDF. Send: "Awesome — I want to make sure this is the right fit for you. Can we jump on a quick 20-min call this week?" The call is where you close. Everything before that is warm-up.

C
Clarify — why are they on the call?
"What made you reach out?" Let them talk. Don't pitch yet. The more they talk the more you understand what they actually need.
L
Label — name their problem back to them
"So it sounds like you've tried the gym before but never had a real structure and kept losing motivation — is that right?" When they hear their problem reflected back, they feel understood. That's trust.
O
Overview past failures without judgment
"What have you tried before? What stopped you?" Make them feel safe to be honest. This surfaces real objections before they become blockers at the end.
S
Sell the vacation, not the plane
Describe the result, not the program. Don't say "you get weekly check-ins." Say "in 12 weeks you'll walk into a room differently. You'll have the confidence you've been waiting to have."
E
Explain — what's included, handle objections
Walk through the offer and guarantee. For price objections: "You've spent years trying free stuff that didn't work. What does another year of the same look like?"
R
Reinforce after they say yes
"You've made a great call. This is exactly the right time to do this." People second-guess purchases. Immediate reinforcement kills buyer's remorse before it starts.
ObjectionResponse
"It's too expensive""What's it costing you to stay where you are? Not just financially — in how you show up every day."
"I need to think about it""What's the specific thing holding you back? Let's talk through it now."
"I'll try it myself first""You've probably already tried that. What would be different this time?"
