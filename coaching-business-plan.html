Section 04 — Growth

Getting
Clients

Hormozi's first dollar came from a personal conversation. Not an ad, not a post. Your first 3 clients are already in your phone right now.

Phase 1 — warm outreach (weeks 1–4, zero cost)

People who've commented on your fitness posts. Mates who've asked how you lost the weight. Anyone who's ever said "you should train me." Goal: message 30 people, convert 3. That's a 10% rate, very achievable with a personal message.

Hey [Name], launching my online coaching and taking on a small group of first clients for my 12-week program. Thought of you specifically because [personal reason — e.g. "you mentioned wanting to sort your training", "I know you've been trying to lose weight for a while"]. I coach young guys on strength training and nutrition — I went from being overweight myself to where I am now, and that's exactly what I build programs around. Taking on only 5 people for this first round to keep it personal. Want me to send through the details?

Why this message works

  • You're not asking for money — just "do you want details?" Low friction yes
  • The specific personal reason makes it feel genuine, not a mass blast
  • Scarcity ("only 5 people") creates urgency without being salesy
  • Your story is the credibility — no need to list qualifications

Phase 2 — referral system (month 2 onward)

When a client hits a milestone — a PR, visible progress photos, real confidence — that's the exact moment to ask. Not salesy, just honest:

"Mate, I'm really stoked with how you're going. If you know anyone who'd benefit from this, I'd love an intro — I'll give you a free month for any referral who signs up."

One happy client who refers two people has covered your marketing cost indefinitely. This is the organic growth engine Hormozi builds everything around.

Phase 3 — inbound via content (month 3+)

Consistent content + a clear offer = inbound DMs asking to work with you. The goal is that within 90 days, people are coming to you instead of you chasing them. See the Content Strategy section for the exact weekly system.

Section 05 — Growth

Sales &
Closing

The CLOSER framework from Hormozi. Not manipulation — a structure that helps people make a decision that's actually good for them.

The discovery call — 20–30 minutes, structured

When someone DMs asking for details, your goal isn't to send them a PDF. It's to get them on a 20-minute call. The call is where you close. Here's the exact framework:

C

Clarify — why are they on the call?

"What made you reach out?" Let them talk. Don't pitch yet. The more they talk, the more you understand what they actually need — and the better you can position your offer.

L

Label — name their problem back to them

"So it sounds like you've tried the gym before but never had a real structure and kept losing motivation after a few weeks — is that right?" When they hear their problem reflected back, they feel understood. That's trust.

O

Overview — explore past failures without judgment

"What have you tried before? What stopped you?" Make them feel safe to be honest. This surfaces the real objections before they become blockers at the end.

S

Sell the vacation, not the plane

Describe the result, not the program. Don't say "you get weekly check-ins." Say "in 12 weeks you'll walk into a room differently. You'll have the confidence you've been waiting to have." Paint the future state.

E

Explain — what's included, handle objections

Walk through the offer and the guarantee. For price objections: "I get it, $997 is real money. But you've spent the last 2 years trying free stuff that didn't work. What does another year of the same look like?"

R

Reinforce — after they say yes

"You've made a great call. This is exactly the right time to do this." People second-guess purchases. Immediate reinforcement kills buyer's remorse before it starts and sets the tone for a committed client relationship.

Handling the most common objections

ObjectionResponse
"It's too expensive""What's it costing you to stay where you are? Not just financially — socially, mentally, in how you show up every day?"
"I need to think about it""What's the specific thing holding you back? Let's talk through it now — I'd rather help you make the right decision than have you sit on it for a week."
"I'll try it myself first""You've probably already tried that. The definition of the same thing getting the same result. What would be different this time?"
"I'm not sure it works online""Every client I've worked with has said that. The check-ins, form videos, and weekly contact often mean more accountability than a PT you see twice a week."

The progression logic

  • Year 1 is about proof. You need case studies, testimonials, and a repeatable system. No shortcuts here. Do the work, get the results, earn the right to scale.
  • Year 2 is about leverage. Group coaching means you're not capped at hours in a day. 40 people in a program at $397/month is $15,880 recurring — for one program delivery.
  • Year 3 is about brand equity. When your content has compounded for 2+ years and your results are undeniable, you become the go-to for your niche in Australia. At that point the business attracts clients on its own.

The honest truth about getting there

None of this happens from optimising your offer page or building the perfect funnel. It happens from doing the uncomfortable thing: starting conversations, showing up with content even when no one watches, and delivering results so good that your clients sell for you. Hormozi's whole model is built on one idea — make the product so good, the marketing takes care of itself.

You have the story. You have the skills. You have the niche. The only variable left is execution.
